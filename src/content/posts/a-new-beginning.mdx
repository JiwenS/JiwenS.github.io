Creating a text-based channel in mid-2026 may not look like the most obvious choice.

Short video is still one of the strongest formats for spreading information. AI has also flooded the internet with polished summaries, repeated claims, and plenty of buzzword salad. At the same time, more people are using AI to summarize long videos and articles before they experience the original material. That can be convenient, but it also compresses context, texture, and depth.

The question I keep coming back to is simple: is writing a positive direction for me?

## Why Write

We are surrounded by information every day. It reaches us through countless channels, formats, algorithms, and intermediaries. Once information leaves its original source, every additional layer of processing creates some loss. It also introduces the perspective and bias of whoever is interpreting it.

Recommendation systems make this more intense. The more we consume views that already match our own, the more similar views get sent back to us. Over time, that loop can turn convenience into an information cocoon.

Writing pushes in the opposite direction. Consuming information is easy. Producing a coherent article is harder. It forces me to weigh words, examine assumptions, check whether an argument can survive scrutiny, and separate what I actually think from what I have merely absorbed.

That is one reason I wanted to create this blog. I hope it will push me to think more deeply, produce more actively, and keep a sharper sense of intellectual independence.

## Why Own the Space

Another reason is that this blog exists independently from any specific platform. It is not shaped by platform incentives, interface constraints, or distribution rules I do not control.

I can write what I want to write, arrange the site how I want to arrange it, and treat the space as something closer to a home than a profile page. It is a small piece of owned infrastructure where I can experiment with form, voice, and direction.

The idea reminds me of the [Colleague Skill project](https://github.com/titanwings/colleague-skill), which turned accumulated personal context into something reusable. If I build a substantial body of work here, maybe some version of me can eventually be distilled into a strange little digital avatar too.

## What Comes Next

For now, I expect this space to move in two directions.

The first direction will focus on finance, product management, AI, and the systems around them. Some posts will come from direct experience, and some will be attempts to understand where the industry is moving.

The second direction will be broader reflection: thoughts on events, viewpoints, books, media, or ideas that feel worth working through in public.

This first post is mostly a beginning. It is a stake in the ground, a note to myself, and a small commitment to keep thinking in writing. In the next post, I plan to share the process behind building and setting up this blog.
