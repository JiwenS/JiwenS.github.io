Many AI products are introduced with a promise that is too large for the actual workflow. They claim transformation, replacement, or autonomy before the user has seen enough reliability to trust the system.

A smaller promise can be stronger. It gives the product a narrower surface area, a clearer success condition, and a faster path to repeated use.

## Reliability is a product feature

For AI products, reliability is not only uptime or latency. It is the user's ability to predict where the system helps, where it struggles, and what kind of review remains necessary.

When that boundary is clear, users can build habits around the product. When it is vague, every interaction becomes an evaluation.

## Narrow does not mean small

A narrow product promise can still support a large business. The important question is whether the product owns a repeated moment of value. If it does, expansion can come from adjacent jobs instead of inflated language.

This is especially important in workflows where mistakes are costly. Trust grows when the product keeps a promise the user can verify.
