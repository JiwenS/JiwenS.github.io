## Core Brand Direction

The brand direction is Product-Minded Analyst.

The site should present the owner as a finance-industry product and development practitioner who uses product thinking to interpret financial systems, technology shifts, and market signals.

## Working Tagline

Product thinking for finance and technology.

## Supporting Tagline Options

- Reading the signals behind finance, technology, and products.
- Notes on products, markets, and technology shifts.
- An operator's view on financial products and technology change.

## Editorial Structure

The brand should use a Signals / Systems / Products structure.

Signals:

Market, investment, technology, and user behavior signals that reveal what may be changing.

Systems:

The financial, technical, business, and infrastructure systems behind visible product or market behavior.

Products:

Product strategy, product teardown, user experience, monetization, growth, and execution analysis.

## Brand Attributes

- Analytical
- Practical
- Current
- Independent
- High-signal
- Product-minded

## Brand Should Feel Like

A sharp English personal publication from someone who works inside financial products, understands technology at a practical level, and studies markets with investor curiosity.

## Brand Should Not Feel Like

- A generic personal portfolio.
- A casual lifestyle blog.
- A daily market news digest.
- A hype-driven AI or fintech commentary feed.
- A corporate thought-leadership page without a personal point of view.

## Homepage Implications

The first screen should quickly communicate:

- Who the owner is professionally.
- What topics the site covers.
- Why the owner's point of view is distinct.
- Where readers should start reading.

The homepage should make the Product-Minded Analyst identity visible through headline, tagline, navigation, and featured content taxonomy.
