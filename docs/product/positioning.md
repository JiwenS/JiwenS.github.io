# Product Positioning

Version: v0.1.0
Last Updated: 2026-05-14
Owner: Human project owner
Status: Draft

## Product Definition

This site is an English-first personal blog and thought platform focused on the intersection of finance, technology, and product.

It exists to help the owner express industry understanding, publish structured insights, and build long-term personal influence.

## Positioning Statement

An English personal channel for thoughtful analysis on finance, technology, and product strategy, written from the perspective of a practitioner building a recognizable professional voice.

## Primary Goals

- Build a credible personal IP around finance, technology, and product thinking.
- Publish ideas that demonstrate industry understanding, judgment, and original perspective.
- Create a durable public home for essays, observations, case studies, and product analysis.
- Develop a content system that can support future distribution through newsletters, social platforms, talks, or professional opportunities.

## Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Product builders, operators, and founders interested in finance and technology.
- Professionals who follow fintech, AI, digital products, markets, and business models.
- Readers who value clear analysis over news aggregation.

Secondary audience:

- Potential collaborators, employers, investors, partners, or peers who want to understand the owner's thinking.
- Readers outside the owner's immediate network who discover the site through search, social sharing, or referrals.

## Content Pillars

1. Finance and market structure
   Analysis of financial systems, fintech products, business models, and market behavior.

2. Technology and AI
   Reflections on technology shifts, AI products, infrastructure, and their business implications.

3. Product strategy
   Product teardown, positioning, user behavior, growth, monetization, and execution lessons.

4. Personal operating notes
   Selective reflections on learning, building, decision-making, and professional growth when they reinforce the core personal IP.

## Non-Goals

- The site is not a general lifestyle blog.
- The site is not a real-time news outlet.
- The site is not a personal diary.
- The site should not publish content only because it is trendy unless the owner has a clear point of view.

## Success Signals

- Readers can quickly understand what the owner thinks about and why it matters.
- Articles compound into a coherent body of work.
- The site becomes a reference point for the owner's professional identity.
- Content can be reused across external channels without losing the central positioning.
